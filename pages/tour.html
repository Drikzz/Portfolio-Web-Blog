DAY ONE - FOUNDEVER PH & GOOGLE PHILIPPINES

#Day_1

Where Classroom Meets Reality: Day One at Foundever PH & Google PH

June 9, 2025 — The opening day of our industry tour unfolded with visits to Foundever PH and Google Philippines, giving us a front-row seat to how innovation drives modern business and public impact. At Foundever, we discovered how AI and data analytics shape customer experiences, explored cybersecurity in operations, and learned how the IT-BPM sector continues to fuel economic growth in the Philippines—all reinforced by insights from IBPAP. Later at Google PH, the conversation shifted to digital inclusion, ethical tech, and how public policy intersects with global innovation. With open lounges and smart workspaces all around, it was more than just a tour—it was a glimpse into how technology, collaboration, and purpose come together to build the future. Day One wasn't just informative—it was transformative.

DAY TWO - TELEPERFORMANCE PHILIPPINES & KOLLAB

#Day_2

Day Two at Teleperformance & Kollab: Innovation, Inclusivity, and Real-World Readiness

June 10, 2025 — The second day of our industry immersion led us into two distinct but equally inspiring spaces: Teleperformance Philippines and Kollab. At TP PH, we learned how human-centric tech meets global service excellence. Ms. Beatrice Grace Bonjibod shared how Teleperformance harnesses innovation and diversity to shape business solutions—with 53% of their workforce being women, TP's commitment to inclusion was front and center. Students toured the Innovation Hub and got practical HR insights, even participating in mock interviews to prepare for real-world roles. Later at Kollab, the energy shifted to AI, workplace culture, and creative development. Experts like Ralph Regalado, Jazmine Calma, Toni Monserrat, and Miguel Siriban showcased everything from applied AI to portfolio building—with honest stories, sharp advice, and encouragement for the future tech leaders in the room. From automation demos to snack chats, Kollab turned theory into inspiration. Day Two wasn't just a visit—it was an invitation to dream, design, and develop with purpose.

DAY THREE - ASIAN DEVELOPMENT BANK (ADB)

#Day_3

Day Three at ADB: Where Tech Meets Purpose

June 11, 2025 — The third leg of the industry tour brought WMSU BSCS students into the heart of mission-driven innovation at the Asian Development Bank exploring the ADB History Gallery to engaging with IT leaders in a fireside chat, the visit gave students a rare glimpse into how global institutions harness technology to solve regional challenges. Highlights included expert advice on working in multilateral environments, a deep dive into the bank's digital strategies, and real stories from professionals in infrastructure and network services. A moment was also given to a student-led presentation of RePay, a financial lending app focused on inclusion and transparency. Its real-world application earned positive attention and sparked discussion on ethical fintech. The visit wrapped up with networking sessions and insights into the ADB Future IT Leaders Graduate Program—a professional development track designed to nurture emerging talents in the field of technology. Day Three left us with clarity, inspiration, and a renewed sense of what innovation can achieve when guided by purpose.

DAY FOUR - MICROSOURCING

#Day_4

Day Three Extended: Unlocking Careers with MicroSourcing

June 11, 2025 — Wrapping up an insightful day, WMSU CCS students visited MicroSourcing, gaining firsthand exposure to the operations of a leading outsourcing solutions provider. Ms. Heidee Enriquez, CEO of MicroSourcing and Beepo, emphasized the power of curiosity, digital skills, and the value of adapting to fast-evolving workspaces. From flexible employment models to global client services, MicroSourcing's mission of empowering Filipino professionals resonated deeply. Students toured a modern facility that blended comfort and collaboration, then joined a dynamic LinkedIn Workshop focused on building a strong digital presence. They learned how branding themselves online can lead to real-world internship and job opportunities. The day ended with genuine interaction, appreciation, and new confidence in how academic training connects with career success. For the BSCS delegation, MicroSourcing wasn't just a visit—it was a launchpad for professionalism and purpose.
